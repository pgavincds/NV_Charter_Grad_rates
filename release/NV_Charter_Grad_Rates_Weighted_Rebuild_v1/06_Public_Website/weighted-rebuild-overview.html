Nevada Charter Graduation Rates: Weighted Sector View

This page is a technical companion to the quality-seats dashboard. It asks a narrower but related question: when Nevada charter graduation rates are reported at the sector level, are they calculated in a way that reflects students rather than schools?

For public presentation, the charts on this page begin in 2014-15. The underlying audit files still retain 2013-14, but that opening legacy year is treated as too fragile to use as the visual baseline.

What This Page Shows

  • Weighted ACGR trend lines rather than average-of-school rates.
  • How many charter students actually graduated each year.
  • How the sector's cohort shifted across brick-and-mortar, virtual, and alternative models.
  • How different weighted and unweighted interpretations can be.
  • Which schools contributed the most graduates in selected years.
  • The public chart window starts in 2014-15, with 2013-14 retained only in the audit layer.

Interpretation Guardrails

  • Only legal charter rows under SPCSA, ASD, Clark, and Washoe are included in charter-slice calculations.
  • Non-charter outliers such as Davidson and Independence are excluded from charter-only estimates.
  • Validation Day enrollment is used to verify grade-12 presence, not as the ACGR denominator.
  • Published Nevada ACGR values control when they conflict with numerator/denominator-derived rates.
