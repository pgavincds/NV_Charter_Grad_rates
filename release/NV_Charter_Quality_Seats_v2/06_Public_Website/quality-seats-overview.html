Nevada Charter Quality Seats

How many charter students were in 1-star, 2-star, 3-star, 4-star, and 5-star schools each year?

This page now shows the recovered legacy years directly in the charts. The earliest years are more directional than the later workbook-based years, but they are visible because they help tell the actual historical story instead of making the sector look like it suddenly appears in the middle of the timeline.

The first charts show stacked sectorwide seat counts by star level over time, then sector mix, and only after that total enrollment context. For consistency across authorizers, school bands are standardized statewide as .1 = ES, .2 = MS, and .3 = HS.

Slide 1

All charter seats by 1-5 star rating

This is the main question: how many students were in low-, middle-, and high-performing charter schools each year?

Read it like a stacked bar chart stretched over time. The bottom layer is one star, then two star, then three, then four, then five. The legacy years are shown for directionality, while 2015-16 and 2021-22 remain omitted because they do not function like standard rating years in this build.

Slide 2

All charter seat share by 1-5 star rating

This shows whether the sector mix is changing, not just whether the sector is getting bigger.

If the upper bands take up more of the chart over time, a larger share of rated charter seats is being served in higher-rated schools. If the lower bands expand, more students are being served in lower-performing schools.

Slide 3

Collapsed view: 1-2, 3, and 4-5

This is the simpler policy version when a five-layer chart feels too busy.

It preserves the same bottom-to-top logic: lower-performing schools sit at the bottom, middle-performing schools in the middle, and higher-performing schools on top. Seats with no quality indicator are still excluded.

Slide 4

Total charter enrollment context by grade band

This is the supporting enrollment view: how the charter sector itself changed across elementary, middle, and high school seats.

Use it to understand the scale behind the rating charts above. The legacy years are shown directionally here as well, while 2015-16 and 2021-22 remain omitted.

Important reading notes

  • 2012-13 through 2014-15 come from legacy state-charter accountability recoveries rather than the later modern workbook pipeline.
  • For multi-band legacy schools, ES/MS/HS seat counts are estimated from the nearest later validation-day band shares because the older PDFs publish school totals but not exact band-level seat counts.
  • 2015-16 is still best understood as a transition year, but it is retained in the download files rather than shown in the visible chart sequence.
  • 2021-22 is omitted from the public chart sequence rather than being shown as a pseudo-comparable rating year.
  • Seats in Not Rated schools are excluded from the main rating charts because they do not carry a usable quality indicator.
  • Grade bands are standardized statewide using the published suffix convention rather than LEA-specific grade-span quirks.
  • When local enrollment workbooks show spillover grades outside the published suffix band, those seats are excluded rather than reassigned.
  • A very small number of rows remain in a manual-review bucket for later research; they are not forced into the published totals.
