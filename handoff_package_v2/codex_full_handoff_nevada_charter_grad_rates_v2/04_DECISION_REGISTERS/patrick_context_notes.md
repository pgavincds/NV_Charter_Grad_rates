# Patrick Context Notes for Codex

Use these as contextual notes, not as source-of-truth records. Validate with public files where possible.

- Nevada charters are created under Nevada charter law. A district-operated choice program or vendor-managed school is not a charter merely because families can choose it or a third-party operator is involved.
- District-created choice/virtual/alternative programs cannot be assumed to enroll statewide like SPCSA/statewide charter schools; cross-district enrollment rules differ.
- Early Nevada charter high-school cohorts were dominated by a few large statewide online schools and alternative schools.
- Coral and NSHS were high-performing but small relative to the online behemoths.
- Many later high-quality K-8 operators had not yet added high-school grades in the early years; part of the policy story was growing the high-school denominator and high-quality high-school seats.
- Be careful with closed schools: once a school closed, do not assume later student outcomes can be attributed back to that charter unless the public data clearly supports it.
- The project should be transparent about limitations without pretending to have exact precision where public records are inconsistent, suppressed, or internally conflicting.
